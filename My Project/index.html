Bio

Roger Federer is a professional tennis player currently ranked 3 by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP). Federer has won an open era record 17 Men's Singles Grandslam titles and was ranked No.1 in the world for a record 302 weeks, including a record 237 consecutive weeks. Federer is considered the greatest tennis player of all time by players, commentators and fans all around the world.

Records

Roger Federer has won an open record 17 Men's singles grandslams. Federer holds the record for the most weeks at No.1 ranking (302 weeks), as well as most consecutive weeks as No.1 (237 weeks). He has won a record 7 Wimbledon, 5 US Open, and 6 World Tour Finals (previously Masters Cup) including record 5 consecutive titles at both Wimbledon and US Open.

Seasons

Federer turned an ATP pro in 1998. He first won his grandslam at the All England Club in 2003. Federer first became ATP World No.1 on February 2004 where he held his spot for a record 237 consecutive weeks. Federer won 3 Grandslams each in the years 2004, 2006 and 2007 as well as reached all four grandslam finals in 2006, 2007 and 2009. His 2006 season when he won 3 Slams (AO, Wim, US), reached the final at the other, 4 Masters 1000, Masters cup and reached the finals of 16 of 17 tournaments he played and a 92-5 W/L record is considered one of the greatest tennis seasons of all time.
